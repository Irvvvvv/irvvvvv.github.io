Who am I?

Hello! My name is Irving Garcia-Gomez, and I'm from Lumberton, North Carolina. I am currently a sophomore at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, majoring in Information Science with a minor in Data Science. My interests include user experience, interface design, and inclusive technology that makes digital tools more accessible to everyone.

My passion for technology began when I was six years old, watching my older brothers play on the PlayStation 2. I remember being captivated by the bright, colorful graphics and display of certain video games. I also remember how simple yet efficient the user interface was for the console, which led to several fun and memorable experiences.

Since then, I've pursued opportunities that allow me to combine creativity, leadership, and community. I currently serve as the Outreach Coordinator for Latinos In Tech (SHPE), where I connect students with professional opportunities and foster an inclusive tech community. I am also a SILS Student Ambassador, representing the School of Information and Library Science, and a Kessler Intern, where I collaborate on initiatives supporting first-generation college students.

Beyond my academic and leadership experiences, I have developed strong technical and design skills. I am proficient in Python, HTML, and CSS for web development, and experienced in design tools like Figma and Canva. These tools allow me to combine creativity with technical ability to create accessible digital experiences that empower communities.
